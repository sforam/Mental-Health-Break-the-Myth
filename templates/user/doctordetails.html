FAQs:

Why did you choose to become a psychiatrist?

I developed a keen interest in human behaviour while pursuing my medical internship. I became aware of the pain that people with emotional difficulties face in their day to day life, and decided to join psychiatry to make a difference in people's lives. I know how difficult it can be to live with mental health concerns. Hence, health and well-being of my clients is my top-most priority. My aim is to ensure that my clients are completely involved in the process of creating a treatment plan for their concerns. By going on a collaborative journey with my clients, I hope to help them achieve their desired goals and find much-needed relief from all their emotional difficulties.

What would you like to tell a client who comes to you for psychiatric support?

Health and well-being of my clients is my top-most priority. I try to ensure that my clients are completely involved in the process of creating a treatment plan for their concerns. By going on a collaborative journey with my clients, I hope to help them achieve their desired goals and find much-needed relief from all their emotional difficulties.

What does a first session with you look like?

The first session is all about getting to know the client, understanding their background and the concerns they have come with. I discuss my professional understanding of their concerns with them. I take time to discuss the available resources to alleviate their concerns and also explain the treatment plan to commence their journey of healing. What has been an important learning of being a psychiatrist? Over the period of my carrer as a psychiatrist and a doctor i have learned that, accomplishments in most aspects of life are dependent upon emotional and mental well-being.
